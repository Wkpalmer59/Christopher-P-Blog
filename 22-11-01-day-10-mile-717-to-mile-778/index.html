November 1, 2022 – Day 10 – Mile 717 to Mile 778

As we prepared to leave Brunswick Marina this morning, we had a friendly Egret sitting on our stern line (see picture).

We left Fernandina Beach at 8:50 AM following the Intracoastal Waterway past the Timiucuan Ecological and Historic Preserve, up Broward Creek to Sisters Creek past the Naval Station at Mayport.

At 9:41 AM we saw the launch of the SpaceX Falcon Heavy USSF-44. As the Kennedy Space Center is still 100 miles to the south, we didn’t see it until it was up in the sky.

We continued down Pablo Creek to the Tolomato River. We went through 6+ miles of mega mansions on the waterway.

We are docked for the night at the St Augustine Municipal Marina. St. Augustine was “founded in 1565 by Spanish explorers, it is the oldest continuously inhabited European-established settlement in what is now the contiguous United States.”

Docked right next to us is the pirate ship “Black Raven.”

The pistol shrimp (or snapping shrimp) can often be heard when we are docked…we also heard them as we walked back along the dock after dinner. They make a distinct snapping noise (snap, crackle, pop). According to Wikipedia, they can interfere with sonar or underwater communication and considered a major source of noise in the ocean.

We head to Daytona Beach next.
