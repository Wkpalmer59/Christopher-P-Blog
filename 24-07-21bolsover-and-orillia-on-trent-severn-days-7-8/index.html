Bolsover and Orillia, ON - Trent-Severn Days 7 & 8

This is a combined post as we have not had good internet access until today.

On Friday we traveled from Bobcaygeon to Bolsover. We did our last “up” lock reaching a total height of 595 feet. The first down lock was a regular lock and then we did the Kirkfield lift lock which lowered us 49 feet. Pretty cool hanging out over the edge.

We then traveled through the narrowest sections we have ever done. They recommend that you put out a “security“ call on the radio to let other boaters know you are coming through so 2 big boats don’t end up bow to bow with no way for both to fit through, but apparently not everyone listens as we met 5 other boats coming the other way and didn’t have fun trying to pass them.

We did see some other interesting sights along the way.

Yesterday we did another 5 down locks in quick succession, then crossed lake Simcoe, which is the largest body of water on the Trent-Severn. We arrived in Orillia and tied up in a large municipal marina with great facilities, but were surprised to see that it was pretty empty. There are a bunch of loopers here and we joined an impromptu Docktails party and met a bunch of other loopers.

Tomorrow we are moving on and will plan to get in line for the main event on the Trent-Severn, the ”Big Chute” marine railway.
