Olmsted Lock and Dam, KY

Today we did our last 40 miles on the Mississippi through a lot of bends and passing several large “Tows” which are tugs pushing many barges. We saw one today with 20 barges.

We left the Mississippi and headed up the Ohio River where we immediately noticed how much clearer & cleaner the water looked after the muddy debris filled waters of the Mississippi. Unfortunately we lost the 2.5 knot current push we had in the Mississippi and now have a 1 knot current against us that we will have until our next destination. We anchored just below the Olmsted Lock which we will go through first thing tomorrow morning.

Tomorrow we have about 30 miles to go after we pass through the lock to reach Paducah where we will spend two nights at a marina. We will be glad to plant our feet on solid ground after 3 nights in a row of not having access.
