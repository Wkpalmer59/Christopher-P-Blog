Clearwater, FL

On Saturday we left the marina in Carrabelle and moved to an anchorage behind Dog Island to stage for an early departure for our Gulf crossing on Sunday. We had a peaceful night and were treated to a nice sunset.

We hauled anchor at 5:30am and traveled in the dark (not fun) out of the inlet and into the Gulf of Mexico. We watched the sunrise to an absolutely perfect day and set a course for Clearwater 144 nautical miles away.

We had some playful dolphins escort us along as they were jumping clear out of the water.

When we were about 20 miles out of Clearwater, we came across a capsized boat. It looked pretty recent and there was no one in sight in distress. We reported it to the Coast Guard and surprisingly they had a helicopter on sight within about 10 minutes. We heard several radio calls referencing it as we continued on giving us the impression they may have been looking for that particular boat.

We arrived in Clearwater around 4pm and were thankful for a mostly calm 11 hour run.
